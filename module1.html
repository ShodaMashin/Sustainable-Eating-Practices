The impacts of our Western diets

Sustainable Eating

What foods we decide to eat has an impact on not only our health, but also our environment. So ... improving the quality of our diet, and also reducing environmental impact, is a major focus all over the world (1).

The Western Diet

The “Western diet” is the commonly adopted diet nowadays. This diet includes a high intake of refined carbohydrates (like pasta, white rice, and pastries), added sugar, sodium, and animal products, paired with a low intake of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

Let's have a look at the current diets of our friends Jordy, Clive, Brianna, and Alexis.

Jordy is a classic example of the Western diet, full of refined carbs like white rice and a high intake of red meat, paired with a small amount of fresh vegetables. This kind of diet presents challenges for both the health of the population and environmental sustainability. In terms of sustainability, it is a major contributor to environmental burdens including greenhouse gas emissions and land use (1). By including some simple swaps into your diet, such as Clive consuming chicken rather than red meat, he saves a whopping 2,323kg of greenhouse gas production each year.
